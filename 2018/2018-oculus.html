Atacama Museum of Astronomy

2014


The Atacama Desert is known to be one of the driest places on Earth. The dearth of moisture and civilization within this region has created an exotic landscape perfect for stargazers and filmmakers alike. An unfortunate side-effect of this popularity is an increasing rate of tourism that tests the ecological balance of this desert. While being one of the most hostile places on Earth, the region has ironically become even more sought after. The project seeks to minimize the impact such a large functional space can force onto the landscape by integrating the museum into the desert plane. The importance such a flat skyline places upon this location is preserved in order to maximize the potential of the unbuilt environment.
