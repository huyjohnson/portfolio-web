Core 3: Housing Studio "Part to Whole"
Columbia GSAPP
Critic: Adam Frampton, Partner: Joey Xu
2019

In the latter half of the 20th Century, modern architects like Alison and Peter Smithson sought new typologies using new brutalist techniques to house low-income families. These experiments ultimately failed due to numerous socio-economic reasons out of their control, yet their wisdom in creating common spaces for communication and collaboration left their mark on future generations of apartment building design.

In this studio, we evaluated and recontextualized the normalized compositions that make up our housing today and attempted to invent and repurpose new parts of housing and new models of aggregation. While the separation of public and private spaces was respected, the singular hallway that wound its way up to the roof brought a new dynamic to the community. Its essence is felt between the public domains of the courtyard neighborhood while allowing for ample privacy when needed. The interactivity of semi-public and semi-private spheres of influence allows for a quintessential element of humanity, which is the sociability of the community.
